Legal Rex: Document Attestation and Apostille Services

Legal Rex offers comprehensive services for document Attestation and Apostille, which are vital processes for authenticating documents for international use.

Service Purpose Requirement
Attestation Verifies the authenticity of documents for international use Required for documents used in most countries (general authentication by local authorities)
Apostille A specialized form of attestation that confirms the document is genuine and can be accepted by other member countries without further authentication Required for documents used in countries that are members of The Hague Convention

The Apostille Process in India

The Apostille process, required for documents to be recognized internationally by Hague Convention countries, can be complex and typically involves multiple steps:

  1. Notary Verification, Attestation and Valid outside India Attestation
  2. State-Level Attestation
  3. Validation from a Designated Authority: In India, this is the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA).
  4. Final Apostille Stamp

Here is a breakdown of the key information, which clarifies the administrative complexity Legal Rex helps clients manage:
The document is often referred to interchangeably as a Bachelorhood Certificate, Single Status Certificate, or CENOMAR (Certificate of No Marriage Record).
Crucially, India does not issue a single, unified government-printed "CENOMAR" document. Instead, the process involves legalizing a self-declared affidavit to make it internationally recognized.

1. Issuing/Attesting Authority

The authority is not a single office but a chain of legalization starting at the local level and culminating at the national level.

Level Authority Action
I (Local) Notary Public or Oath Commissioner Drafts and notarizes the affidavit (initial legal validation).
II (District/State) Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) or Competent Court Verifies the affidavit and provides an official stamp/attestation. This is the core local verification step.
III (National) Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), New Delhi Attests or issues an Apostille sticker (Hague Convention countries) to validate the document for international use.
IV (International) Destination Country's Embassy/Consulate Provides the final Embassy Attestation (required for non-Hague Convention countries).

2. Required Documentation (Commonly Required)

To prove one's unmarried status to the SDM and MEA, the applicant must typically submit:

  • Single Status Affidavit: The core document, which is a self-declaration of unmarried status, prepared by a lawyer or notary.
  • Proof of Identity: Valid Passport (mandatory, including first and last pages).
  • Proof of Address/Jurisdiction: Aadhar Card, Voter ID, Utility Bill, etc., to confirm the SDM's jurisdiction.
  • Proof of Birth: Birth Certificate or Matriculation (school leaving) Certificate.
  • Parental Documents (Optional but Recommended): A letter/affidavit from parents affirming the single status, along with copies of their ID proofs.

üìù Special Cases:

  • Divorcee: Original Divorce Decree (Judgement of Dissolution of Marriage) must also be attested.
  • Widow/Widower: Original Death Certificate of the former spouse must also be attested.
